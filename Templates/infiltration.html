Infiltration Attack

About

Infiltration attacks refer to the unauthorized entry of malicious entities into a secure environment, often with the goal of gaining access to sensitive information or compromising the integrity of a system.Techniques used in infiltration attacks may include exploiting vulnerabilities in software, utilizing social engineering, or employing advanced persistent threats (APTs) to maintain long-term unauthorized access.

Impact

Infiltration attacks can lead to data breaches, unauthorized access, loss of intellectual property, and compromise the overall security of an organization's network.

Mitigation

Organizations can defend against infiltration attacks by regularly updating software, implementing strong access controls, conducting security awareness training, and using intrusion detection and prevention systems.
