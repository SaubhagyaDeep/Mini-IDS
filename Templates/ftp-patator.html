FTP-Patator Attack

About

FTP-Patator is a type of brute-force attack specifically targeting FTP (File Transfer Protocol) servers. In this attack, an attacker systematically tries to guess usernames and passwords to gain unauthorized access to an FTP server.

Impact

Successful FTP-Patator attacks can lead to unauthorized access to sensitive files and data stored on the compromised FTP server.

Mitigation

To defend against FTP-Patator attacks, it is essential to implement strong password policies, use account lockout mechanisms, and regularly monitor and log FTP server activities.
